Genentech — Imaging Workflow in Python

Python-based image analysis workflows for Genentech’s Department of Research Pathology — automating multiplex tissue imaging at scale, deployed in an HPC environment, cutting processing time by ~30%.

Genentech Imaging Scientist Intern Jun – Aug 2023 South San Francisco, CA Computational Pathology

Overview

As an Imaging Scientist Intern in Genentech’s Department of Research Pathology (Jun–Aug 2023), developed and implemented Python-based image analysis workflows for multiplex tissue imaging. The project automated core preprocessing and ETL steps — tissue segmentation, marker intensity normalization, data source integration, and compartment classification — reducing imaging data processing time by approximately 30%.

The workflows were deployed in Genentech’s high-performance computing (HPC) environment, enabling accurate detection of histologic features across diverse multiplex tissue image types at scale. Multiple data sources were consolidated into unified, research-ready datasets to support downstream biomarker quantification and oncology research.

Pipeline Stages

01

Tissue Segmentation

Applied Otsu-based thresholding on the PanCK channel to delineate tissue regions from glass and background. Multiple thresholding strategies were evaluated side-by-side to maximize tissue recall across diverse multiplex image types.

02

ETL & Multi-Source Data Integration

Designed ETL patterns to ingest immunofluorescence imaging data from multiple sources and consolidate it into unified, research-ready datasets. This standardized formats, resolved cross-source inconsistencies, and eliminated manual preparation steps — a core driver of the ~30% efficiency gain.

03

PanCK Intensity Normalization & Outlier Trimming

Computed 3D surface distributions of PanCK pixel intensity across the full tissue area. Identified and trimmed high-intensity outlier pixels (dashed cutoff in the surface plot) to ensure downstream marker quantification is not dominated by saturated regions.

04

Compartment Classification & HPC Deployment

Generated pixel-level tissue masks classifying each region as Tumor (PanCK-positive), Stroma (tissue-positive, PanCK-negative), or Background. The full pipeline was deployed on Genentech’s HPC cluster, enabling batch processing of large image cohorts for the Research Pathology team.
